Mission

We are entering a post-AI era where access to information is no longer scarce. Answers are cheap. Explanations are instant. What’s becoming rare—and therefore valuable—is judgment, context, curiosity, and human guidance. Education can no longer be about transmitting knowledge alone. Its purpose must shift toward helping people learn how to think, what to build, and who to become.

My work is driven by a belief that the future of education lies in mentorship over instruction, projects over credentials, and community over isolation. AI can accelerate learning, but it cannot replace the formative experiences that come from struggling through real problems, receiving thoughtful feedback, and being seen by someone who believes in your potential. In a world where machines can generate solutions, humans need spaces to practice discernment, creativity, and responsibility.

I’m building a startup that reimagines learning as an apprenticeship model for the modern world. Small groups of learners are paired with experienced mentors to work on authentic, portfolio-worthy projects—research, products, writing, tools—that matter beyond the classroom. AI is not treated as a shortcut or a threat, but as a collaborator: a tool that frees learners to go deeper, think bigger, and move faster, while mentors focus on higher-order guidance.

This model is intentionally human-scaled. Learning happens through conversation, iteration, and shared effort—not standardized pacing or anonymous assessment. Progress is measured by growth, clarity, and impact, not test scores. The goal is not to optimize for compliance or prestige, but to cultivate confidence, agency, and a sense of purpose.

Ultimately, my vision is for an education system that prepares people not just for jobs, but for meaningful participation in a rapidly changing world. One where learners don’t ask, “What should I memorize?” but “What can I contribute?” In a post-AI era, the most important thing education can do is help people become thoughtful builders of the future—together.
